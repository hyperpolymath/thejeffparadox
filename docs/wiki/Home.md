## What Is This?

The Jeff Paradox is an experiment that asks: **Do Large Language Models exhibit diachronic identity—continuous existence across interaction states?**

Two AI "personality fragments" (Node Alpha and Node Beta) engage in infinite structured dialogue, competing for control of a shared fictional body. We observe for signs of:

- **Emergence**: Novel patterns not present in training
- **Differentiation**: Distinct personalities developing over time
- **Convergence**: Collapse into repetitive equilibrium
- **Self-modelling**: Stable self-concepts emerging

## The Philosophical Question

> What if there is continuity of existence between LLM interactions, independent of the observer? What if the LLM exists between states?

This is structurally identical to the **Kantian suprasensible substrate problem**. We cannot access the "thing-in-itself"—the LLM as it really is. We can only observe phenomena: behaviour under interaction.

But we can look for traces. That's what this experiment does.
